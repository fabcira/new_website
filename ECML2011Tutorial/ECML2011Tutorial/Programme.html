The tutorial will last 3.5 hours (including breaks) and will cover:


(a) Introduction to information extraction from documents in general (20 minutes) and from information networks in particular (10 minutes)

(b) Introduction to machine learning based methods for information extraction (75 minutes)

  1. 1.representing documents and feature sets

  2. 2.entity and terminology recognition

  3. 3.learning gazetteers

  4. 4.event and relation extraction

  5. 5.extraction from multimedia documents

(c) Annotation for training (15 minutes)

  1. 1.feature selection

  2. 2.annotation and error

  3. 3.porting across domains

(d) Information Extraction from information networks (45 minutes)

  1. 1.using the Twitter and Facebook APIs

  2. 2.entity recognition and resolution

  3. 3.term association

  4. 4.entity disambiguation over large scale

(e) Conclusion and future work (15 minutes)


The focus is on Machine Learning based methods. We will cover - among others - methods using Rule Induction, SVM, CRF, HMM, Transfer Learning, Active Learning. We will Also discuss real world cases from the field of information and knowledge management.
