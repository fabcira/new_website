Research Area

I coordinate the OAK Group at the department of computer science. We focus on knowledge and information management over large scale. The research covers 3 main areas:

  • How to capture data and information over large scale (the Web, the Social Web, distributed organisational archives, mobile devices, drones, sensors, etc.) with a particular focus on sensing using mobile phones.
  • How to model and understand the collected data and information via data and visual analytics
  • How to use the captured information (e.g. for knowledge management, business intelligence, customer analysis, management of large scale events via social media, etc.) with a particular focus on data analytics.
  • How to communicate the information (to final users, problem owners, etc.).

Research areas covered are knowledge and information management, Web technologies, human computer interaction, search technologies and large scale analysis of social media data.

My research income since 2006 tops £9 million, of which around 20% directly from industry (e.g., Rolls-Royce plc, JustGiving, Football Whispers, etc.). I have co-created two spin-out companies : K-Now Ltd (specializing in knowledge management via social media) and The Floow (a company providing car telematics solutions for insurance to hundreds of thousands of users on four continents).

Application areas and success stories:

  • Sensing via mobile phones, wearables and low cost architectures. I have developed:
    • Tracking via Mobile Phones: I have created a human mobility tracking technology with around 900,000 downloads in the UK, used for supporting governmental campaigns for health and wellbeing, as well as to understand mobility in large metropolitan areas. For example this wass used for three years in Active 10 on behalf of Public Health England. This was an app and a large scale server infrastructure designed to collect sub-minute tracking data from over 900,000 users. The same tracking technology was used by the Birmingham City Council to track thousands of free bikes with the Seta App. It was also used by MoveMore a project that aims to make Sheffield the city in the Uk that "moves more" by 2020. Currently the technology has been released to our new spin out Aeqora and implemented into:
      • The Aeqora App, an app released to four hospitals in Germany, UK, Israel and Italy as part of the Mobilise-D EU project
      • the The Corona Tracker Risk Modeller, ap app to support the fight to the Corona Virus pandemic
    • tracking of items in hospitals. We have a project funded by the Rotherham general Hospital
    • Tracking heart conditions and the correlation with mobility in patients using wearables and mobile phones
    • EyesOnTheGround: a method to turn any citizen camera into a camera for the emergency control room. The citizen will receive a text message with a link. Clicking on the link will share the camera with the control room. The control room will be able to communicate with the citizen via bidirectional audio, control the citizen phone (switching between front and back camera), identifying the exact citizen position via the phone GPS, as well as recording the video interaction and taking screenshots and notes. For situation where installation of an app in advance is feasible, I have developed also the corresponding Android and iOS apps. This technology is now finding application in hospitals to support occupational therapists.
    • SeeingWithMyOwnEyes: a Web tool to support visually impaired students during lectures (it provides easy real-time screen sharing between lecturer and student via a web browser)
    • Several other mobile technologies such as a noise detector, an OBDII tracker, etc.
    • Impact of this activity has been considerable: we have spun out two companies:
      The Floow providing telematic services to some of the major international insurers
      Aeqora, providing services for tracking humans and items in smart cities, with a particular focus on heath and wellbeing

  • Situation Awareness via social media (Facebook, Twitter, etc.) and the Web. We develop methods for large-scale information capture of structured and unstructured data. Particular focus on use for emergency response during natural catastrophes and social unrest.

    • We have developed the TRIDS framework for situation awareness in events using social media.
    • It has been used by emergency control rooms in UK and Europe to monitor and manage large events involving over a million people over two years, inlcuding some fo the largest music festivals in the world and the evacuation of 30,000 people from the city of Vicenza (Italy) to support the defuse of a world war II bomb.
    • We have been supporting Football Whispers in the analysis of around 70 million social media messages a month since 2015.
    • Relevant projects: WeSenseIt (EU), Lodie (EPSRC), Randms, (ESPRC/MoD), Trids (TSB/SBRI), WeKnowIt, EA horizon scanning (Environment Agency)
  • Behaviour Analysis in real life and in the digital world: we have been working on user modelling and Profiling of Web and real world users. Areas of application:
    • Monitoring domestic energy consumption patterns;
    • Deception in the digital world: behaviour analysis of social media user to discover bot attacks, spamming, smear campaigns, and astroturfing
    • Monitoring driver behaviour: creation of a spin-out company (The Floow Ltd) developing and commercializing a product for driver behaviour tracking using mobile phones. Adopted by largest UK insurer and the 3rd largest insurer in USA. Currently under deployment in the UK, Argentina, India and Singapore.
    • Relevant projects: Big Energy Upgrade, Randms, (ESPRC/MoD).
    • Large Scale Knowledge Management in Large Organisations: we develop methodologies to support organisation in organising and reusing their knowledge and data. Main highlights:
      • Overall coordination of the X-Media EU Project on large-scale knowledge management across media, €13.8m, 15 partners, £1m of follow-on investment by industrial partners, 3 spin-out companies created.
      • Creation of models for large scale indexing and searching of distributed company archives.
      • Created a terminology recognition a system for aerospace currently, certified for Aerospace use , currently core to
        Rolls-Royce knowledge management programme (patent pending);
      • Creation of a spin-out company (K-Now Ltd), 10 employees, over 200k/year income commercializing our search and indexing technology.
      • Relevant projects: Siloet, Samulet, Smartproducts, X-Media, Dot.Kom, Ipas
