One studentships is available to work on Web-related techniques for Knowledge Management. The projects will concern study, development and application of Semantic Web methodologies and are likely to be associated to some more general work done in cooperation with large companies in the fields of aerospace and/or pharmaceutical research.

Applications are welcome from people with an education in computer science, with at least a 2:1 degree (or equivalent).  We expect the candidates to have good track record, including excellent programming skills in Java and Web technologies. We look for people highly motivated in doing research at the highest levels and in entering a multidisciplinary team.Knowledge of Information Extraction from Text, Machine Learning, Human Computer Interaction and Natural Language Processing are definite advantages.

Sheffield's NLP group is one of the internationally leading groups for research on Information Extraction and NLP in general.

See the Web Intelligent Technologies pages for a description of the current activities relevant to the bursaries.

The Bursary:

One bursary will be associated with the AKT project, a multi million pound project for research on the future of knowledge management funded by EPSRC. AKT extends knowledge management technologies to exploit the potential of the semantic web, covering the use of knowledge over its entire lifecycle, from acquisition to maintenance and deletion.  Partners involved in this project are the University of Sheffield, Southampton, Edinburgh, Aberdeen and Open University.

More information on Akt can be found at www.aktors.org.

Eligibility, Stipend and Duration

As both the studentships are funded by EPSRC, the usual rules for elegibility and stipend hold, i.e.:

  • People who have been living in the UK for at least three years (with no restriction on nationality) (student visas not acceptable) can apply for the full studentships that will include:
    • a stipend of UK £12,000 per annum
    • paid University fee
  • European citizens
    • paid University fees (no stipend!)
  • Others:
    • not eligible

The post is offered for three years.

This is a lively and busy academic department currently with twenty-seven academic staff, twenty-five research associates, one departmental administrator, six secretaries, seven technical staff and approximately 500 undergraduate and postgraduate students in the Department.   We are in both the Faculty of Engineering and the Faculty of Pure Science running six undergraduate degree courses and five MSc courses.   There are six research groups – Natural Language Processing, Speech and Hearing, Verification and Testing, Machine Learning, Graphics and Robotics.

The NLP Research Group is the largest group within the Department of Computer Science, with 4 Professors, 1 Readers, 1 Senior Lecturer 2 lecturers and 1 Senior Research Scientist.  The group also consists of about 40 among Research Associates and Ph.D. Students, and includes 2 Research Coordinators, and 1 Research Secretary as well.

The NLP Group currently has 11 Grants, 5 European (1 as Coordinator) and 7 UK.
